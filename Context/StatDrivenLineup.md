# The Stat-Driven Lineup Structure

Each batting order spot has a defined role. Players are assigned to spots based on their cumulative season stats, not gut feel. The goal is to maximize run production by putting the right bat in the right situation.

---

| Spot | Role               | Primary Stat     | Purpose |
|-----:|:-------------------|:-----------------|:--------|
| 1    | The Catalyst       | OBP + Speed      | Gets on base to start rallies. Highest probability of reaching in the first at-bat of the game. |
| 2    | Best All-Around    | OPS              | Most plate appearances of any top hitter. Drives in runs and gets on base consistently. |
| 3    | High-Value Bat     | OPS              | Hits with runners on base; high probability of extra-base hits when it matters. |
| 4    | The Cleanup        | OPS / RE24/PA    | Maximizes RBI potential. Usually comes up with runners already on from spots 1–3. |
| 5    | Second Cleanup     | OPS              | Protects the 4-hole hitter. Keeps the pressure on — pitchers can't pitch around #4 with #5 lurking. |
| 6    | Second Lead-off    | OBP + Speed      | Starts rallies for the bottom half of the order. Mirrors the role of the #1 hitter. |
| 7    | Mid-Range          | OPS              | Balanced hitter who bridges the gap between the top and bottom of the order. |
| 8    | Lower Tier         | OPS              | Often a defensive specialist. Keeps an out from happening right before the top of the order turns over. |
| 9    | Table Setter       | OBP + Speed      | Acts as a second lead-off, setting the table for spots 1–3 in the next inning. |

---

## How Spots 2–4 Are Filled

Spots 2, 3, and 4 are filled **as a group** rather than one at a time. The three players with the highest RE24/PA (excluding the player already in spot 1) form the *core group*. Within that group, OPS determines the order:

- **Spot 2** — highest OPS in the core group
- **Spot 3** — next highest OPS
- **Spot 4** — remaining core group player

This ensures all three prime run-producing spots are occupied by the team's most impactful bats, rather than letting a high-OPS but low-RE24/PA player push a more impactful hitter down the order.

---

## Tiebreakers

When two players are tied on the primary stat for a spot:

1. **Speed** (higher wins) — applies only at speed-sensitive spots: 1, 6, and 9
2. **RE24/PA** (higher wins) — applies at all spots
3. **PA** (higher wins) — larger sample size as final tiebreak
